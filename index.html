Historical Monuments of India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, situated in Delhi, was the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is known for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 73 meters.

India Gate

The India Gate, an iconic war memorial in New Delhi, commemorates the Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, located in Jaipur, is a palace known for its unique honeycomb-like structure and was built for the royal women to observe street festivals unseen.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India, situated in Mumbai, was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.
